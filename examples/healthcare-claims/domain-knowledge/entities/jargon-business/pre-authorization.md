# Pre-Authorization

## Definition

Requirement for providers to obtain approval before delivering certain services. The plan evaluates medical necessity against clinical criteria. Also called prior authorization or prior auth.

## Context

Pre-authorization applies to high-cost procedures, specialty medications, advanced imaging, and elective surgeries. The pre-auth decision (approved/denied/not-required) is stored and referenced during adjudication. Claims for auth-required services without matching approval are denied with reason code PA-DENY.

Auth-required lists are maintained per benefit plan and updated quarterly. Standard authorizations are valid for 90 days, surgical authorizations for 60 days.

## Example

Physician requests pre-auth for MRI. Utilization review team evaluates clinical docs against criteria. If approved, authorization number issued with approved units and validity period. When MRI claim is later submitted, adjudication matches it against stored authorization.
