# Out-of-Network (OON)

## Definition

Provider/facility without a contract with the health plan. OON services have higher cost-sharing: higher deductible, higher coinsurance, and allowed amount based on fee schedule benchmarks rather than negotiated rates.

## Context

During adjudication, the rules engine checks provider network status via the provider directory. OON allowed amount is typically calculated as a percentage of Medicare rates or a UCR (Usual, Customary, and Reasonable) benchmark.

Providers may balance-bill the member for amounts above the allowed amount. Some plans have no OON benefits except for emergencies.

## Example

OON specialist charges $500. Plan's OON allowed amount is $300 (UCR). Plan pays 60% ($180). Member owes 40% ($120) plus $200 balance = $320 total.
