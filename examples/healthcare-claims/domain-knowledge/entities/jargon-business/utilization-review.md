# Utilization Review

## Definition

Clinical evaluation where licensed reviewers assess whether services are medically necessary and appropriate. Types: prospective (before service, tied to pre-auth), concurrent (during inpatient stay), retrospective (after service, during adjudication).

## Context

Utilization review decisions directly impact claims processing. Prospective reviews feed pre-authorization decisions. Concurrent reviews determine the number of covered inpatient days. Retrospective reviews are triggered during adjudication for unusual patterns.

Clinical criteria sources include InterQual or MCG guidelines, which provide evidence-based standards for medical necessity determinations.

## Example

Hospital submits claim for 7 inpatient days post-surgery. Concurrent review determined criteria supported 4 days. Plan pays 4 days at allowed amount, denies 3 as not medically necessary.
