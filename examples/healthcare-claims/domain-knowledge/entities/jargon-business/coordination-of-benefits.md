# Coordination of Benefits (COB)

## Definition

When a member has multiple plans, COB determines primary (pays first) vs secondary (pays second). Combined payments must not exceed the allowed amount.

## Context

COB uses standard determination rules: subscriber vs dependent, birthday rule for children's coverage, active employment vs COBRA. The primary plan processes claims normally, and the secondary plan covers the remaining member responsibility up to its own benefit limits.

COB data is maintained in the eligibility system. COB scenarios are a major source of claims errors and overpayments, requiring careful coordination between plans.

## Example

Member is employee under Plan A (primary), spouse under Plan B (secondary). Plan A pays $4,000 of $5,000 allowed amount. Plan B pays remaining $1,000. Member owes $0.
