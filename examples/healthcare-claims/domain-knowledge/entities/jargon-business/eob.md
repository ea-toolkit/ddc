# Explanation of Benefits (EOB)

## Definition

Statement generated after adjudication explaining how a claim was processed: billed charges, allowed amount, plan payment, and member responsibility.

## Context

An EOB is not a bill — it is an explanation of how the claim was processed, but it tells the member what they may owe the provider. EOBs are generated by the payment engine after adjudication completes. They are available via the member portal and mailed to the member's address on file.

EOBs include reason codes for any denials or adjustments, helping members understand why certain charges were not covered or were reduced.

## Example

EOB shows: Billed $500, Allowed $350, Plan Paid $280, Deductible $0, Copay $30, Coinsurance $40, You May Owe $70.
